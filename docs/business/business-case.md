# TaxOS — Business Case

**Audience:** Tax Technology leadership (Partner/Director), CFO sponsor, platform investment committee
**Ask:** fund productisation of the governed agentic-execution layer for tax compliance
**Basis:** Phase 1 discovery (full analysis: [docs/discovery](../discovery/README.md)); figures are illustrative models, stated as such

## 1. The problem, in one paragraph

Enterprise tax functions convert scarce professional judgement into data preparation. At a representative 1,000-entity group, 50–70% of tax-team capacity is consumed by extraction, reconciliation, and manual computation; risk is found retrospectively; audit response is archaeology; and the regulatory direction of travel (MTD, e-invoicing mandates, Pillar Two) makes the manual model structurally non-viable within this decade. The market sells compliance *forms* (ONESOURCE, CCH) and research *copilots* (Blue J); nobody has shipped **governed autonomous execution** — the layer that does the work under human control with evidence attached.

## 2. The product thesis

TaxOS is that layer: multi-agent AI that carries compliance work from ERP extract to review-ready state, on top of a deterministic computation core, with evidence generated by default. Three properties make it defensible rather than demo-ware:

1. **Trust is architectural.** Human approval gates, hash-chained audit, deterministic engines, cited AI — the governance that enterprise tax *requires* is built into the write path, not promised in a policy PDF. (This is the adoption blocker for every generic-agent competitor.)
2. **Content is data.** Jurisdictions and tax types are signed content packs; agents are registry configurations. Marginal cost of scope expansion is content authoring, not engineering.
3. **Operations become training data.** Every reviewer disposition and correction is a governed label; the platform's models improve as a by-product of use — a compounding moat no point-in-time tool has.

For a Big Four operator the second model dominates: **managed-service margin** — one platform, many clients (multi-tenant with provable isolation), converting engagement hours into recurring platform revenue. SAM ~$6bn growing ~11% CAGR ([sizing](../discovery/01-business-problem-and-market.md#3-market-opportunity)).

## 4. Why now / why us

Regulatory digitisation is forcing structured tax data (the raw material) · agent frameworks crossed production-readiness in 2024–25 (the capability) · tax authorities already run AI at scale (the asymmetry to close) · and the build demonstrated here de-risks the hard part: the reference implementation exists — architecture, governance model, agent estate, and evaluation harness are designed and documented to internal-asset standard ([18 ADRs](../architecture/README.md#adr-index)).

## 6. Risks & mitigations (top five)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Enterprise trust in AI for tax | The architecture *is* the mitigation (approval gates, determinism, citations, audit chain) — sell the governance, demo the evidence pack |
| Incumbent response | Speed + architectural head start; incumbents carry legacy per-module platforms; partnership is also a valid exit |
| Tax content correctness & maintenance | Packs cite sources; regulatory monitoring flags drift; content team model is the Big Four's existing muscle |
| Model/LLM cost & behaviour volatility | Budget hierarchy, model routing, eval gates on every change, provider abstraction |
| Key-person concentration (build phase) | The documentation corpus is the mitigation — 11 phases, ADRs, runbooks: the bus factor is on paper |

## 7. The ask

Sponsor a 2-quarter productisation of R1 with one design-partner client group, success-gated on: live VAT cycle in production, ≥60% prep-hour reduction measured, evidence-pack acceptance by the client's auditor, and a signed second client. Decision artifacts — demo, architecture walkthrough, security programme — are ready today.
