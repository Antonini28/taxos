# Executive Presentation — Content Spec

**Purpose:** the 15-minute Partner/CFO deck. This document is the authored content; the rendered `.pptx` (styled per the Meridian system) is produced in Phase 13 from exactly this outline. Rule: one idea per slide, every claim traceable to the corpus, screenshots from the live demo only (no mockups presented as product).

---

**1. Title** — *TaxOS: the tax function that runs itself — under your control.* Live product screenshot (executive dashboard, dark mode) as backdrop.

**2. The problem** — Your tax team's week (stacked bar: 55% data wrangling / 25% computation / 20% judgement — illustrative, sourced note). Punchline: *the judgement is why you hired them.*

**3. The forcing function** — Regulatory timeline graphic (MTD → e-invoicing mandates → Pillar Two). *Authorities are digitising faster than corporates. HMRC already runs AI on your data. You're analysing yours in Excel.*

**4. The market gap** — 2×2: execution↔advice vs manual↔autonomous. Incumbents (forms), copilots (answers), in-house scripts (fragile) plotted; the empty quadrant labelled **governed autonomous execution** — *nobody ships this. This is TaxOS.*

**5. What it does (the demo slide)** — The flagship flow in 5 frames: instruct → agent plan → escalation handled → draft with citations → human approval + evidence pack. *(Presenter cue: switch to live demo here if time allows — `just demo`, 3 minutes.)*

**6. Why you can trust it (the differentiator slide)** — Four locks, four icons: **Humans approve everything** (agents architecturally cannot file) · **AI never does the maths** (deterministic, reproducible engine) · **Every claim is cited** (or refused) · **Every action is chained** (tamper-evident audit, exportable evidence). Footer: *this is the slide our competitors can't show.*

**7. What the CFO sees** — Executive dashboard screenshot annotated: compliance heat map, exposure, cash-tax forecast with intervals, drill-to-evidence. *Board question to answer: two clicks, not three weeks.*

**8. What the risk lead sees** — Fraud centre screenshot: ranked cases, plain-language "why flagged", disposition loop. *Anomalies found at ingestion, not at year-end. Every score explains itself.*

**9. Value model** — Single bar: £0.8–2.2m/yr indicative value per group vs low-£100ks platform cost ([business case §3](business-case.md)); second bar for the firm: managed-service margin, one platform × N clients.

**10. How it's built (one architecture slide, no more)** — The 60-second diagram + three captions: *modular core, isolated AI runtime, one auditable write path.* Credibility strip: 18 ADRs · invariant test suite · quarterly DR rehearsals · ISO/SOC2-mapped.

**11. Security & compliance posture** — STRIDE-modelled, injection catalogue tested in CI, GDPR-reconciled (erase the person, keep the evidence), UK-specific: SAO/CCO support built in. *Security review starts from our documents, not from a questionnaire.*

**12. Roadmap** — Four trains on one line: R1 UK VAT vertical (done/reference) → R2 intelligence (RAG, CT, risk ML) → R3 enterprise (multi-tenant, regulatory monitoring) → R4 scale (jurisdiction packs, public API). *Jurisdictions are content, not code.*

**13. The ask** — Business case §7 verbatim: 2 quarters, one design partner, four success gates. Timeline graphic with decision point marked **today**.

**14. Close** — Product screenshot + one line: *Agents prepare. Humans decide. Everything is evidence.* Contact + demo QR.

**Appendix slides (pull as asked):** A1 competitor table · A2 agent roster · A3 evidence-pack sample pages · A4 cost model detail · A5 eval-harness results · A6 team/skills map.
